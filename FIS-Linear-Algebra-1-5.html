Problem 1.5.9
Let V be a vector space and let u, v ∈ V be distinct vectors. Suppose that {u, v}
is linearly dependent. Then by deﬁnition u = αv for some α ∈ F, so that u is a
multiple of v. By symmetry, v is also a multiple of u. Conversely, suppose that,
without loss of generality, u is a multiple of v. Then v = αu for some α ∈ F
and hence {u, v} is linearly dependent by deﬁnition. ■
