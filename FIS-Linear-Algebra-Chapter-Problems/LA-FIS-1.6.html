Let V be a vector space. We will show by contrapositive that if V is innite
dimeensional, then V contains an innite linearly independent subset. Suppose
that V does not contain an innite linearly independent subset. Then any lin-
early independent subset of V is necessarily nite. Hence any basis for V is
5
necessarily nite as well, since it is a linearly independent subset. Thus by de-
nition V is a nite dimensional vector space. Hence by contrapositive, if V is an
innite dimensional vector space, then it necessarily contains an innite linearly
independent subset. Conversely, suppose that V contains an innite linearly in-
dependent subset. Now suppose by way of contradiction that a nite subset
is a basis for V . However we see that then the basis contains less vectors than
the innite linearly independent subset, contradicting the linear independence
of the innite subset. Hence V cannot have a nite basis set and therefore V is
innite dimensional.
