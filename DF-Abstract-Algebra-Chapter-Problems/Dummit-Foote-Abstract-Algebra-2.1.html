Problem 2.1.13
Let H ≤ (Q, +) such that for all nonzero x ∈ H, 1/x ∈ H. Since H is a sub-
group of the additive rationals, we know that 0 ∈ H. If H does not contain any
other elements, then H = 0. We know that H ⊆ Q by deﬁnition of a subgroup.
Now suppose q ∈ Q. We know that 1/q ∈ Q also, and since the choice of q was
arbitrary, we see Q ⊆ H. =⇒ H = Q. Hence for such a subgroup H, we know
that either H = 0 or H = Q. ■
