Problem 1.5.9
Let V be a vector space and let u, v V be distinct vectors. Suppose that {u, v}
is linearly dependent. Then by denition u = v for some F, so that u is a
multiple of v. By symmetry, v is also a multiple of u. Conversely, suppose that,
without loss of generality, u is a multiple of v. Then v = u for some F
and hence {u, v} is linearly dependent by denition.
