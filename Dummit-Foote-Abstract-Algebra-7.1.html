Problem 7.1.3
Let R be a ring with identity and let S be a subring of R, with 1 ∈ S. Suppose
that u ∈ S is a unit. Then there is s ∈ S such that without loss of generality
us = 1. Since u, s, 1 ∈ S ⊆ R, then the equality holds in R and so u is a unit in
R. ■
For an example to show that the converse is false, consider the ring Z, which
is a subring of Q. Since Q is a ﬁeld, all nonzero elements are units, but the only
units in Z are ±1.
