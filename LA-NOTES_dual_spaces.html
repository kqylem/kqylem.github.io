Dual Space Theory

It's time to learn what a matrix transpose really means. We know that there is a 1-1 correspondence between $\mathcal{L}(V,W)$ and $M_{m\times n}(\mathbb{K})$. But what does the transpose of a matrix mean in the context of linear transformations?

Definition 1: Let $V$ be a vector space. Then the vector space $\mathcal{L}(V,\mathbb{K})$ is called the dual space of $V$, also denoted by $V^{*}$.
