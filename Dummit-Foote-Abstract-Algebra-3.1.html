Problem 3.1.3
Let A be an abelian group and B ≤ A. Since A is abelian, B is a nor-
mal subgroup (see Example 2). Therefore B is the kernel of some homomor-
phism by Proposition 7, speciﬁcally, the natural projection homomorphism,
such that π : A → A/B deﬁned by π(a) = aB. Let aB, cB ∈ A/B, then
aB · cB = (ac)B = (ca)B = cB · aB since A is abelian. Hence A/B is also an
abelian group as desired. ■
