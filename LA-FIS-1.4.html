Problem 1.4.11
Let v ∈ span({x}). Then v is some linear combination of x, namely a multiple
of x. Hence v ∈ {ax : a ∈ F} =⇒ span({x}) ⊆ {ax : a ∈ F}. The converse
follows trivially in that the set on the right is the set of all multiples of x, iden-
tical to the span of x. ■
Problem 1.4.12
Suppose that W ⊆ V is a subspace of a vector space V . Since W is a subspace of
V that contains W ⊆ V trivially, then by Theorem 1.5, span(W ) ⊆ W . By deﬁ-
nition of span, we see that any for any w ∈ W , w ∈ span(W ), so W ⊆ span(W ),
and hence span(W ) = W . Conversely, suppose that span(W ) = W . By The-
orem 1.5, the span of any subset of V is a subspace of V . Hence W ⊆ V is a
subspace of V as desired. ■
